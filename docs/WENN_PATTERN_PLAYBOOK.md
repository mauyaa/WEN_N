# WENN Pattern Playbook

## Purpose
Turn shared trading notes into a disciplined research playbook for WENN.

This playbook does not treat patterns as magic.
It treats them as structured context that can improve timing when combined with market state, volatility, volume, and risk filters.

## Core rule
A candlestick pattern alone is never enough.
WENN should only care when a pattern appears in a meaningful location and under acceptable market conditions.

## Pattern families to support in v1
### Bullish reversal candidates
- bullish engulfing
- demand-zone rejection
- strong lower-wick recovery candle

### Bearish reversal candidates
- bearish engulfing
- shooting star
- evening star
- dark cloud cover
- supply-zone rejection

## Location matters more than shape
WENN should only elevate a pattern when it appears near:
- a defined demand zone
- a defined supply zone
- higher-timeframe support/resistance
- a key moving average context
- a liquidity sweep / failed breakout area

## Bullish engulfing playbook
### Minimum context
- appears after visible downward pressure or a pullback inside a larger uptrend
- second candle fully engulfs the prior real body
- volume should not be weak
- market must not be in invalid/high-volatility guardrail state

### Stronger confirmation
- next candle closes above the engulfing high
- signal forms near demand or support
- volume ratio is elevated
- higher timeframe is not bearish

### WENN interpretation
A bullish engulfing pattern is not a buy command.
It is evidence that selling pressure may have weakened and demand may be regaining control.

## Bearish engulfing playbook
### Minimum context
- appears after upward pressure or a rally into supply
- bearish candle fully engulfs prior bullish real body
- pattern is not isolated from structure

### Stronger confirmation
- next candle closes below the engulfing low
- appears near supply or resistance
- upper-timeframe bias is not strongly bullish

## Shooting star playbook
### Minimum context
- long upper wick
- small real body near the candle low
- appears after price pushes up into a sensitive area

### Interpretation
Buyers pushed but could not hold control. WENN should only elevate this when structure and location agree.

## Evening star playbook
### Minimum context
- strong bullish candle
- smaller indecisive candle next
- bearish follow-through candle after

### Interpretation
Momentum is stalling and may reverse. Use only when the third candle confirms weakness.

## Dark cloud cover playbook
### Minimum context
- first candle bullish
- next candle opens strong but closes deeply into the first candle body

### Interpretation
Upward momentum is being challenged. WENN should treat this as a cautionary reversal candidate, not a standalone command.

## Supply and demand layer
WENN should store simple zone logic:
- demand zone = area where strong rejection upward previously began
- supply zone = area where strong rejection downward previously began

### Zone strength factors
- number of clean rejections
- speed of departure from the zone
- recency
- whether the zone has been revisited multiple times

## Confirmation stack
A pattern becomes relevant only when several layers align:
1. location quality
2. market state quality
3. volume/participation quality
4. volatility not excessive
5. acceptable reward-to-risk
6. no guardrail block

## Scoring idea
Suggested pattern scoring buckets:
- 0.0 to 0.39 = ignore
- 0.40 to 0.59 = weak observation only
- 0.60 to 0.74 = candidate under review
- 0.75+ = strong research candidate

## What WENN should avoid
- pattern trading in noisy ranges without context
- pattern trading during invalid feed state
- treating every wick as meaningful
- treating educational examples as universal law

## Recommended v1 implementation
Start with:
- bullish engulfing
- bearish engulfing
- simple supply-demand zone tagging
- volume confirmation
- next-candle confirmation rule

Add more patterns only after the first set is stable.

## One-line principle
**WENN should trade the story around the pattern, not the pattern in isolation.**
